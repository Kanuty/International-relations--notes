[[China]]
[[Mao Zedong]]
[[Defensive Realism]]
Adoption of defensive posture but it may conduct offensive operations (They are not willing to conquer or coerce foreign states.... unless they do not see them as foreign like for example Taiwan)

sources: On Protracted War, Problems of Strategy in China's Revolutionary War.

Originally it was primarly land doctrine/strategy (Mao was focusing on actions on the ground), lter it got updated to include maritime competiiton, aerospace, cyber and informational domains.

This doctrine is the example on non nuclear [[Deterrence]]
It also focuses on a [[Perception of Threat]], to present yourself as the defensive and non aggressive.
The deffensive actions may still apear ofensive. Buildup of the naval forces, initialy thought as the shore and maritime lines defense forces could be viewd as a challange thrown against other naval states.

So here we have something drastically opposite to what we could see in potential doctrine of the polish state, when we dould like to be seen as potentially agresive (we will strike Russia/Belarus even if not attacked directly but if we will think that our vital interests are in danger... yet we are not going to just invade teritories for sake of it so maybe our doctrine is the active defense with extra steps???) 
The question is, when state should use the Active Defence doctrine and when it is not beneficial?

The active defense require from a state to be able to absorb initial strike, to have teritorial depth, high resistance to attrition and be able to perform mass mobilization.

The doctine changed based on international situation because originally it was thought mostly as the civil (revolutionaly) war doctrine, then world war (III) doctrine and now it evolved into regional/limited doctrines.

A lot of states are using the 'active defense' as theirs posture. They are just using different laungage. France with theirs [[Force de Frappe]] are basically implementing the Active Defense with a nuclear twist. Japan is also close, however they are more close to passive defense (which is, in practice, huge difference!) 

Active Defense require some level of strategic autonomy. If the US can shut down your own capabilites, then active defense is impossible. Even if they will allow you, initially to behave independently it is only the case because you are fullfiling theirs wishesh/objectives.

That is why, for example Poland and Germany are unable to use active defense strategy.
